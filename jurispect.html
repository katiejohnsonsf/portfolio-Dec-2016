Jurispect
  • Research
  • Design
  • Testing

Jurispect is a legal informatics company that helps make state and federal financial regulation actionable to financial compliance teams. After 20+ listening sessions with relevant professsionals and team workshops, the team helped to synthesize research findings into UX principles and a resulting hypothesis to test for each.

Note: I've blurred these artifacts to protect of IP of the company.


team persona: Starting with interviews, we organized observations from from our research by role and a 9-step organizational process using Stickies.io and later Mural.ly. The deliverable included measurable UX principles upon which to measure the success of our design.






scenarios: We focused our initial prototype on a core set of functionality in the orgranizational process needed to accomplish the professional goals of the people we interviewed. We selected three scenarios from this portion of the team persona.



task analysis: Once we had specfic user contexts in mind for our first iteration, we broke down each scenario into discrete steps.

We went through several design iterations after testing sessions. We started out combining users flows into a site map. We sketched wireframes using the team persona, scenarios and site map individually and in a team workshop with the goal of testing each UX principles through our solutions. Using the team feedback, I created wireframes and collected early client feedback. Based on the client feedback, I began creating the prototype in Sketch and setting up for user testing with the client.

Note: I've blurred these artifacts to protect of IP of the company.


user flows: We used the task analysis for each scenario into steps as a user flow.




site map: We combined the user flows into a site map to get a sense of the overall architecture of the product.



wireframe sketches: We sketched on paper and whiteboard, individually and in workshop to define the best interface to support the user goals.



workshop for design critique and sketching: We workshopped to discuss how three sets of sketches for each scenario compared. The team presented their analysis of the designs with the scenario.



medium fidelity mockups: After showing sketches to the client, we were ready to create the first version of the prototype using Sketch and InVision.

User feedback was a primary challenge for this project since the market is a group of people whose use of the product would take place in highly secure and formal organizations - making informal and frequent feedback difficult. However, we managed to have two successful testing sessions where we built upon our knowledge of the problem and hypotheses for the solution.

Note: I've blurred these artifacts to protect of IP of the company.


testing script: We selected details from a story one of the test particpants had relayed to get a more realistic impression of how our solution fit their needs. Team members took notes for faster task creation the same day. We also recorded the session as a back up.
