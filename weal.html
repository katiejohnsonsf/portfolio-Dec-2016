Weal
  • Research
  • Design
  • Testing

Weal is an early-stage social enterprise that already had a rich body of research in the care taking tool space. As a UX design consultant, I guided and supported Weal's strategy and process to translate the team's knowledge into a viable product that would take the company to their next fundraising benchmark.


research synthesis workshop: We kicked off the project with a workshop to externalize the team's assumptions about their personas and the dynamics that take place between each.

Note: I've blurred these artifacts to protect IP of the company.




concept model: We visually summarized the system we hoped to facilitate through the product by bringing together research for each of the three personas.



user flow: After selecting our initial scenarios and resulting task analysis based on its core functionality, we created a user flow for each persona.



Building on our synthesis of research and resulting artifacts, we discussed each persona, scenario, user flow and interaction. We explored possibilities for every aspect of the interface on the whiteboard, made a decision for the final approach then used Proto.io to build a prototype.

Note: I've blurred these artifacts to protect IP of the company.

whiteboard sketching session: We sketched individually then discussed the pros and cons of each approach, labeling each with a persona, scenario and tasks to make sure our sketches captured everything.



site map:The Site map allowed us to combine all screen flows into one, cohesive product.


medium fidelity prototype: We used Proto.io to build a prototype that would allow us to test our design hypotheses.



Using Proto.io to test in-person and remotely, the team learned to facilitate testing sesssions, synthesize feedback and evolve our design hypotheses to build confidence for the company's next product launch and funding round.


testing script: We created a testing script for each persona that would feel relatable and cover all of the core scenarios.
